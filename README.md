## Fraud Detection Analysis ##
### Overview

Analyzed 284,807 real-world credit card transactions to detect fraudulent activity patterns. The dataset represents two days of transactions by European cardholders in September 2013. The goal was to identify key fraud patterns including transaction amounts, timing of fraud activity, and the overall scale of financial impact - providing actionable insights for fraud detection teams to prioritize monitoring and prevent future losses. Out of 284,807 transactions, only 492 were fraudulent (0.17%) - making this a highly imbalanced real-world dataset typical of financial fraud scenarios.

## Key Findings

- 492 fraudulent transactions detected out of 
  284,807 total (0.17% fraud rate)
  
- Fraudulent transactions totalled $60,128 compared 
  to $25,102,462 in legitimate transactions
  
- Fraud activity peaks at specific hours of the day 
  indicating organized fraudulent behaviour
  
- Average fraudulent transaction amount differs 
  significantly from legitimate transactions
  
- Despite low fraud rate, financial impact justifies 
  dedicated real-time monitoring systems
